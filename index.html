Ashlee Mirizio

PhD Student in Informatics | Computing, Culture, and Society

I am a PhD student in Informatics within the Computing, Culture, and Society track at the Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering. My work explores how sociotechnical systems shape and are shaped by cultural, organizational, and institutional contexts.

Broadly, my research interests include technology ethics, qualitative and mixed methods research, and critical perspectives on computing. I am particularly interested in understanding how design and policy decisions influence equity, participation, and power in digital systems.

Outside of my academic work, I enjoy staying active through CrossFit.
