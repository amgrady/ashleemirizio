Ashlee Mirizio

PhD Student in Informatics @ Indiana University

Focus: Computing, Culture, and Society

I am a PhD student in Informatics at the Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering at Indiana University, Bloomington. I bring a background in technology and business, having previously worked in a technology company and as a management consultant.

Broadly, my research examines the multiple and varied intersections between computing, culture, and society, and the ways in which these things shape one another. More specifically, I am interested in Silicon Valley sub-cultures, their constructions and perceptions of risk, and how these groups navigate uncertainty.

My current work explores the methods these sub-cultures use to translate or “solve for” risk, with particular attention to artificial intelligence (AI) and technological predictions of the future.

Outside of my academic work, I both participate in and coach CrossFit.
